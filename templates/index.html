Disaster Information

Floods

Floods are natural disasters caused by the overflow of water onto land that is usually dry. They can result from heavy rainfall, river overflow, melting snow, or dam breakage. Floods can cause widespread damage to property and infrastructure and lead to loss of life.

Earthquakes

Earthquakes are sudden shaking or trembling of the ground caused by the shifting of Earth's tectonic plates. They can lead to significant destruction, especially in densely populated areas, causing buildings to collapse and triggering landslides or tsunamis.

Hurricanes

Hurricanes are powerful tropical storms with strong winds and heavy rainfall. They form over warm ocean waters and can cause severe damage through flooding, storm surges, and wind destruction. The impact of hurricanes can extend hundreds of miles inland.

Preparedness Tips

Floods

  1. Stay informed: Monitor weather updates and alerts.
  2. Prepare an emergency kit with essentials (food, water, medications).
  3. Elevate electrical appliances and important documents.
  4. Have an evacuation plan and know the nearest shelters.
  5. Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.

Earthquakes

  1. Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy furniture).
  2. Secure heavy furniture and objects to prevent them from falling.
  3. Prepare an emergency kit with essentials.
  4. Learn how to shut off gas, water, and electricity.
  5. Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" drills regularly.

Hurricanes

  1. Stay informed about the hurricane's path and potential impact.
  2. Secure your home by boarding up windows and reinforcing doors.
  3. Stock up on non-perishable food, water, and emergency supplies.
  4. Have a plan for evacuating if necessary.
  5. Avoid coastal areas and move to a higher elevation if required.
